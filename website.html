LIBRARY RULES FOR READERS

  1. These rules supersede all previous rules or instructions including those which are printed on the library cards and book-card pockets. The library is to be treated as a facility for borrowing/consulting/referring/E-accessing etc. of required information. Students should not mind security check of their belongings by the library staff. Library is for the service of all. No one should misuse it for serving one’s own ends only.
  2. Student may borrow 2,4 and 6 reading material (2 books for Undergraduate, 4 books for postgraduate / M Phil and 6 books for PhD students) at a time. 1st year Undergraduate students will get one Interfaculty card to borrow reading material from other libraries.
  3. Before entering in the library kept your bag on the property counter at your own risk, except money, jewelry, and other valuable things. Library will not be responsible for any theft.
  4. In case of loss of Reader Ticket the following action will be taken by the student –
    a) Immediately report the lost card to the librarian.
    b) Duplicate Reader Ticket will not be ordinarily issued.
    c) Any material issued on the lost card will be charged from the student.
  5. In case of loss of material the following action will be taken by the student -
    a) Report the lost material to the librarian.
    b) Try to replace the material within a week of information.
    c) If one fails to replace the material he/she must deposit the current price plus late fine if any.
  6. In case of any suggestion, difficulty or complaints first approach the concerned person, then section in-charge then Librarian, however, if not satisfied he/she may drop their complaint in the Library’s suggestion/complaint box.
  7. If any reader found his/her requirement for a particular information, book, journal etc. he/she may drop their requirement in the Library’s suggestion box.

    8. ABOUT US

    The KCC library is the leading library body representing the interests of library and information services and their users. It is the national voice of the library and information profession. Libraries are rightly called the store house of valuable knowledge. The library has a separate reading section, reference section and stacking section. The unmatched quality and quantity of material available is also supplemented by courteous and competent staff for facilitating students. The institute has constituted a separate library committee that selects and ensures the regular availability of latest books and reading material in the library. The provision of open access e-resource through internet no longer confines a student to the library only. The library at KCC is well equipped and has an immense collection of resources in form of CD-ROM, online database, books, audio/video CDs, reference books, national and international periodicals, magazines, journals and pool of research work of eminent scholars.
